An outbreak caused by the SARS-CoV-2 Delta variant (B.1.617.2) in a secondary care hospital in Finland, May 2021 

 

Iivo Hetemäki1,2 , Sohvi Kääriäinen3,4 , Pirjo Alho1 , Janne Mikkola1 , Carita Savolainen-Kopra4 , Niina Ikonen4 , Hanna Nohynek4 , Outi Lyytikäinen4

An outbreak caused by the SARS-CoV-2 Delta variant (B.1.617.2) in a secondary care hospital in Finland, May 2021. Euro Surveill. 2021;26(30):pii=2100636. https://doi.org/10.2807/1560-7917.ES.2021.26.30.2100636Received: 28 Jun 2021;   Accepted: 28 Jul 2021

 

The severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) Delta variant of concern (Phylogenetic Assignment of Named Global Outbreak (Pango) lineage designation B.1.617.2) has been suggested to be more transmissible than the Alpha (B.1.1.7) variant [1], which is more transmissible than the wild-type SARS-CoV-2 virus [2-4]. We describe here an outbreak caused by the Delta variant that originated from one inpatient in a secondary care hospital and spread within the hospital and to three primary care facilities; we describe our experiences in controlling it. Cases were detected among patients, healthcare workers (HCW) and in the community. Both symptomatic and asymptomatic infections were found among vaccinated HCW, and secondary transmission occurred from those with symptomatic infections despite use of personal protective equipment (PPE).
